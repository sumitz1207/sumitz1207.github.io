Correlations Between Healthcare Gap and Poverty Levels

As we can see in the above graph, there is a notable relationship between lack of healthcare coverage and poverty. In states with high levels of poverty, particularly in the Deep South and Appalacian region, we see a corresponding high level of lacking healthcare coverage. Conversely, we see low levels of poverty and healthcare defiencies in the more northern states, especially the New England region. This would suggest that high levels of poverty are a barrier for state citizens in achieving healthcare coverage, especially in states lacking universal healthcare policies. Some states that stand out in the data include Texas, which appears to have the highest lack of healthcare coverage despite a mid-level income level. New Hampshire appears to have the lowest level of poverty, but is outdone by states like Massachusetts in healthcare, perhaps due to healthcare mandate policies.
